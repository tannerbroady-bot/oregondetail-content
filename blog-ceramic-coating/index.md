## What Is Ceramic Coating?

Ceramic coating is a liquid polymer applied to a vehicle's painted surfaces that chemically bonds with the clear coat and cures into a hard, semi-permanent protective layer. Unlike wax or sealant, ceramic coating doesn't sit on top of the paint — it bonds to it at a molecular level.

The result is a surface that is dramatically more resistant to environmental contaminants, UV radiation, water spots, and light scratches. Water beads off the surface rather than sitting on it. Paint stays cleaner longer and is far easier to wash.

## Is Ceramic Coating Worth It in Oregon?

Eugene and Springfield's specific combination of conditions makes ceramic coating particularly valuable:

- **Heavy tree canopy** — Douglas fir, oak, and maple sap is highly acidic and will etch unprotected clear coat
- **Wet winters** — prolonged moisture exposure causes water spotting from mineral-rich tap water and road grime
- **Pollen season** — Willamette Valley pollen is some of the heaviest in the country, bonding to paint and requiring aggressive washing that damages unprotected finishes
- **Summer UV** — Oregon summers are dry and UV-intense, accelerating paint oxidation and fading

Ceramic coating directly addresses all of these threats. For most Eugene drivers who plan to keep their vehicle for 3+ years, the math works strongly in favor of at least a Level 1 installation.

**Can ceramic coating be applied to any vehicle?**
Yes — cars, trucks, SUVs, motorcycles, boats, RVs, and classic cars. The process and pricing vary by vehicle size and condition.

**Does ceramic coating prevent rock chips?**
No. Ceramic coating is not thick enough to absorb physical impact. For rock chip protection, paint protection film (PPF) is the correct product.

**How do I maintain a ceramic-coated vehicle?**
Regular washing with pH-neutral soap. Avoid automatic car washes with brushes. Annual maintenance visits are included with all Oregon Detail Co ceramic coating packages.
